Status Quo (Pre-Engagement)
50-person customs ops team manually processing BOLs, commercial invoices, packing lists, classification, and customs filings. Per-shipment cycle 3–5 hours. Customer SLA frequently missed during peak season.
The Problem

Five Customs Jurisdictions. Five Different Schemas. One Stretched Team.

The customs ops team was the bottleneck. Each declaration required a human to read 4–6 documents, cross-check data, classify HTS codes, file with the appropriate authority, and respond to exceptions. Three to five hours per shipment.

Different jurisdictions made it worse. CBP wanted ACE filing format with specific tariff classifications. EU wanted CDS format with different validation rules. China wanted Customs Single Window with paper backups. APAC was a patchwork. Reviewers specialized by jurisdiction, which meant capacity imbalances during peak.

The customer SLA was 24-hour customs clearance for premium shipments. Pre-engagement, they hit it 78% of the time during normal volume — and dropped to 50% during peak season. Premium customers were churning to competitors.

Our Approach

Multi-Format Ingestion + HTS Classification Agent + Multi-Authority Filing

Four pillars carrying a declaration from email attachment to filed customs entry — with a Temporal workflow keeping exceptions tracked through to resolution.

01

Multi-Format Document Ingestion

BOL, commercial invoice, packing list, certificate of origin, dangerous goods declaration. Layout-aware OCR with format-specific extraction templates.

02

HTS Classification Agent

Multi-step classification using product description, materials, country of origin, intended use. Confidence-scored with edge cases routed to specialists.

03

Multi-Authority Filing Layer

Adapters for CBP ACE, EU CDS, China Customs Single Window, India ICEGATE, ANZ ABF. One internal data model, jurisdiction-specific output.

04

Exception Workflow Orchestration

Temporal-based long-running workflow for exceptions. Customs queries, document re-requests, customer follow-ups all tracked through to resolution.

Lessons Learned

01
Per-Jurisdiction Adapters Are the Unsexy 40%
Per-jurisdiction adapters were the unsexy 40% of the engagement. Each jurisdiction took 2–3 weeks of integration. Skipping any meant losing that market.
02
HTS Classification Needs Confidence Routing
HTS classification is a deep specialist skill — automated 91%, but the 9% edge cases needed human review with confidence-scoring routing.
03
Long-Running Workflows Are Essential
Long-running workflows for exceptions (Temporal) were essential. Exceptions can take days; classic request-response doesn't fit.
04
Churn Impact Was the Surprise Win
Customer churn impact was the surprise — internal ROI was the cycle time, but the SLA hit rate moved the business case.
