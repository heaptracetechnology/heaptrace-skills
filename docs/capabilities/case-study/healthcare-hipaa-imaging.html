The Problem

Studies Backlogged. Urgent Cases Mixed With Routine. Compliance Anxiety Every Quarter.

With 50K studies a month and 14 radiologists, the queue routinely backed up. Urgent findings sat behind routine reads. The triage process was first-in-first-out, which is the wrong default for clinical work — and everyone in the practice knew it. The medical director had been asking for a better answer for two years.

Existing AI vendors offered “imaging AI” but came with vague compliance posture, no audit trail, and a black-box decision model that the medical director refused to put in front of patients. The vendor demos always looked great. The procurement reviews always fell apart over data residency, BAAs, and the inability to inspect what the model was actually doing on a per-study basis.

On top of that, quarterly HIPAA audits were a 3-week scramble across the engineering and compliance teams. Evidence collection lived in shared drives. Audit trails were assembled retrospectively from logs that didn't quite match. The compliance officer had flagged this as an enterprise risk in two consecutive board reviews.

Why This Model
Healthcare AI work is never “one and done” — model drift, regulatory changes, and evolving clinical workflows all require sustained engineering attention. The client needed a team that would still be there in month 14 when modality coverage expanded, not a vendor handing off a system and disappearing.
Our Approach

Multi-Modal Triage with Audit Trail at Every Step

Four phased pillars, designed up-front with the medical director and compliance officer in the room. Nothing went into production without sign-off from both.

01

HIPAA-First Architecture

PHI never leaves the client's VPC. All processing on-prem GPU cluster. PHI redaction at every interface boundary. BAAs in place with all sub-processors before a single study was ingested.

02

Multi-Model Vision Pipeline

DICOM-native ingestion. Specialized models per modality — CT, MRI, X-Ray, Ultrasound — each tuned and validated separately. Confidence scoring with thresholds for urgent escalation.

03

Document Intelligence for Reports

Prior-report ingestion to give radiologists context the moment they open a study. RAG pipeline with citation back to the source report — never a paraphrased summary the radiologist has to verify.

04

Audit-Grade Logging

Every decision logged with model version, input hash, confidence score, and radiologist override (if any). Audit trail maps directly to §164.312 technical safeguards. Inspectable in seconds.

Lessons Learned
  1. 1
    PHI redaction must be engineered into the data flow, not added at the end. Retrofitting redaction is where compliance audits get expensive.
  2. 2
    Active learning loops with radiologist override feedback improved model accuracy 12% over 12 months. The override signal is the most valuable training data in the system.
  3. 3
    Quarterly compliance audits are predictable; engineering for them in CI saves 80% of the prep time. Audit evidence should be a build artifact, not a manual collection effort.
  4. 4
    Multi-modal fusion (combining DICOM imagery + prior reports) outperformed any single-modality approach. The radiologist's mental model is multi-modal — the system has to be too.
