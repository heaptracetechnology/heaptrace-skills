The Problem

Manual QA at Production Speed Was a Lost Cause.

Visual QA on a moving production line is one of the hardest jobs in manufacturing. Cycle time per component was 4–7 seconds. Inspectors burned out, missed defects, and turned over fast. The training pipeline couldn't keep up — by the time someone hit peak performance, they were already on their way out.

The economics were cruel. The OEM customer charged $200–$1,200 per defective component shipped. Chargebacks ate 4–7% of margin on this product line. Adding more inspectors was already past the diminishing-returns point — new hires couldn't be trained fast enough to offset the turnover, let alone improve catch rate.

Existing "AI vision" vendors offered cloud-based inference. The plant network couldn't move multi-megabyte images per component to the cloud and back in 4–7 seconds. Latency killed the option before the economics did. The client needed something that fit inside the cycle — on the line, at the station, in real time.

Our Approach

Edge Inference + Active Learning + MES Integration

Four pillars that turn a vision model into a production quality program.

01

Edge-Deployed Inference Pipeline

NVIDIA Jetson AGX deployed at each station. Custom-tuned vision models compiled with TensorRT. Sub-200ms inference per component including image acquisition. Zero round trips to the cloud — the inference fits inside the cycle window.

02

Multi-Class Defect Detection

14 defect classes specific to interior trim — scratches, color variance, dimensional issues, contamination, mold flow lines. Per-class confidence thresholds tuned with the quality team so each defect mode could be biased toward catch or precision based on cost.

03

Active Learning Loop

Borderline-confidence samples and customer-discovered defects flagged back to a Label Studio queue. Quality engineers labeled weekly batches; models retrained monthly. Every customer escalation became a training signal — the system gets harder to fool every week.

04

MES Integration

Real-time defect events streamed into MES with full provenance — image, model version, confidence, station ID. Quality dashboards updated live for each line. Quality engineers can drill from a chargeback back to the exact component, station, and inference that should have caught it.

Lessons Learned

What We Took Away

Four things we'd flag for any team considering a similar program.

Lesson 01
Edge inference is the only architecture that works on production lines. Cloud inference is a non-starter for cycle-time-bound applications — no amount of optimization moves megabytes round-trip in seconds.
Lesson 02
Active learning loops compound. Month-18 accuracy was meaningfully better than month-1 with no architecture change — just feedback. The loop, not the model, was the long-term asset.
Lesson 03
Quality team trust is built one false-positive at a time. Tuning per-class thresholds with the quality team was the highest-leverage 2 weeks of the engagement — without it, line operators would have routed around the system inside a month.
Lesson 04
The chargeback economics were 4x better than projected. Customer-discovered defects had a hidden multiplier in customer relationship damage that didn't show up in chargeback dollars alone — and that was where the real ROI lived.
