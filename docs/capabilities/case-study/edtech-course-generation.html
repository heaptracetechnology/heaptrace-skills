The Problem

Backlog Bigger Than Capacity. Multi-Language Made It Worse. Customers Churned to In-House Solutions.

The training platform competed on catalog breadth. Customers picked it over competitors because they could find a course on virtually any compliance or training topic. But the backlog of requested-but-not-yet-built courses had ballooned to 2,000 — and at 50/quarter throughput, that was a 10-year backlog.

Multi-language was worse. SMEs cost $8K per course per language. A course in 12 languages cost $96K. The economics didn't work, so multi-language coverage was patchy, and large enterprise customers were losing patience.

The platform tried "ChatGPT-generated courses" in a pilot — and SMEs rejected 90% of the output. Generic AI couldn't match the structure, depth, or domain accuracy customers paid for.

Our Approach

Multi-Agent Course Generation + SME Review Loop + Localization Pipeline

Four pillars built and validated against real SME feedback — not lab benchmarks.

01

Course Generation Pipeline

Course-outline agent → module generation → assessment generation → multimedia script generation. Each stage gated by structural validators that block malformed output before it reaches a SME.

02

SME Review Workflow

Generated courses landed in a SME review queue with diff views, suggested edits, and approval-with-feedback. SME feedback flowed back into prompts — a continuous learning loop, not a one-shot model.

03

Localization Native

Courses generated in source language, then localized via the Localization capability with cultural adaptation and TMS integration. Not bolted on as a translation step — designed into the pipeline from day one.

04

Brand & Quality Gates

Custom evaluators for tone, accuracy, structural completeness, and assessment quality. Courses below threshold auto-routed back for regeneration before they ever entered the human queue.

Lessons Learned

01
SME review IS the product
Engineering for the SME experience — diff views, suggested edits, feedback loops — matters more than raw model quality. The pilot before us failed not because the model was bad, but because the review tooling was nonexistent.
02
Continuous learning loops compound
Month-12 generation quality was 30%+ better than month 1 with no model changes — just feedback flow from the SME review queue back into prompts. Compounding gains came from the loop, not the model.
03
Localization native, not bolted on
Designing localization into the pipeline from day one was a 5x cost saver vs translating after generation. Cultural adaptation happened at the generation layer, not as a post-process — which is where most "AI translation" projects fail.
04
Cost economics shifted competitive position
When cost-per-course dropped 85%, the platform started winning RFPs they couldn't before — especially deals with custom-content requirements and multi-language scope. The engagement changed sales motion, not just operations.
