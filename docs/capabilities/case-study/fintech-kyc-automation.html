The Problem

Growth Outpaced Operations. Compliance Fragmentation Across Markets.

Onboarding 50K customers/month with an 80-person ops team meant each reviewer cleared 25-30 cases/day. Quality varied by reviewer, market, and time-of-day. Regulators in two markets had already flagged inconsistencies — the kind that don't trigger fines on the first occurrence but absolutely do on the second.

The fragmentation was worse than the volume. Each of 12 markets had its own KYC requirements — different document types, different sanctions screening lists, different source-of-funds rules, different retention windows. Reviewers specialized by market, but staffing imbalances meant some markets had multi-week backlogs while others sat idle. Hiring more reviewers wasn't a fix — it was a moving target chasing 15% monthly volume growth.

Customer drop-off was the silent killer. 22% of applicants abandoned the flow during the wait. Each abandonment was paid CAC down the drain — marketing dollars that drove a customer to apply, then watched them lose patience and never return. The CFO had calculated the annual leakage at roughly $8M and rising.

Our Approach

Document Intelligence + Agentic Risk Scoring + Per-Market Routing

Four pillars, each engineered for the regulated, multi-jurisdiction reality of cross-border fintech.

01

Multi-Format Document Pipeline

Passport, ID card, driving license, bank statement, utility bill — across 12 markets and 8 languages. OCR + structured extraction + verification API calls (sanctions, PEP, address validation). Layout-aware parsing for documents that vary by issuing country.

02

Agentic Risk Scoring

Multi-factor risk model combining document validity, sanctions hit, source-of-funds plausibility, and behavioral signals from the application flow. Each factor produces a confidence score with reasoning — not a black-box verdict.

03

Per-Market Routing

Automated cases auto-decided. Edge cases routed to a human reviewer with market-specific expertise. Confidence threshold tuned per market based on regulatory expectation — UK lighter, two APAC markets stricter, US calibrated to FinCEN guidance.

04

Audit Trail per Verification

Every decision logged with model version, input documents hash, sanctions list version, decision rationale, and reviewer ID where applicable. Satisfies SR 11-7 model risk and regional banking auditors. Reproducible runs — we can replay any 6-month-old decision.

Lessons Learned
1
Tuning confidence thresholds per market beats one-size-fits-all. The same model output that's auto-decidable in the UK needs human review in two APAC markets — not because the model is worse there, but because the regulatory tolerance for false negatives is different.
2
Sanctions list updates need versioning baked into the audit trail. A decision made on yesterday's list and a decision made on today's list can produce different verdicts for the same applicant — and a regulator will eventually ask which list version was active at decision time. Bake it in.
3
SR 11-7 model documentation is the longest tail in regulated AI — engineer it from day one. Retrofitting model risk documentation onto a shipped system is an order of magnitude more expensive than producing it as you build. Treat it as a deliverable, not paperwork.
4
The conversion-rate impact of faster onboarding paid for the engagement 4x in year one — before counting any ops headcount savings. Compliance was the ticket to entry; conversion was the financial story.
