Why Healthcare

AI in Healthcare Is a Compliance Problem Disguised as a Technology Problem

Healthcare AI vendors love demos but break under audit. Real production requires HIPAA §164 compliance, BAA chains, breach notification readiness, PHI handling at every layer — none of which are afterthoughts.

Add to that FDA's Software-as-Medical-Device pathway for any system making clinical recommendations, multi-state regulatory variance, payer-specific workflows, and the cost of a single misclassification — clinical AI is not a place to learn on the job.

Our healthcare engagements are run by engineers who have lived through HIPAA audits and FDA submissions. We know what to do, and just as importantly, what to never do.

Compliance Ready

Regulations We Engineer Around

Compliance is a system property, not a checklist. Here are the four regulatory frames we design around at architecture time — not at audit time.

01

HIPAA §164

Privacy Rule (164.500), Security Rule (164.300), Breach Notification (164.400). Mapped at every audit point — with technical, administrative, and physical safeguards reflected in the architecture.

02

HITECH Act

Enhanced enforcement, breach reporting timelines, and meaningful-use alignment. Built into incident response flows — with notification clocks, evidence preservation, and chain-of-custody from day one.

03

BAA-Ready Engagements

Business Associate Agreements with covered entities. PHI handling agreements at every step — including subcontractor BAA chains for downstream processors and infrastructure providers.

04

FDA SaMD Pathway

Software as Medical Device classification awareness. We know when an engagement crosses the line into clinical decision support and what that requires — predicate analysis, 510(k) considerations, design controls.
