Public-Sector AI Has to Survive Audit, FOIA, and the Front Page

Government AI is reviewed by inspectors general, audited by GAO, scrutinized by privacy advocates, and ultimately judged by the citizens it serves. Vendors who optimize for "ship fast" ship things that won't survive any of those reviews.

Add FedRAMP for cloud workloads, FISMA for federal systems, Section 508 for accessibility, regional and state-level requirements, air-gapped deployment options for sensitive missions, and a multi-year procurement cycle that demands documentation up front.

Our public-sector engagements ship with security documentation, accessibility verification, and citizen-privacy controls as first-class deliverables. We engineer for the audit, because in public sector the audit always comes.

Compliance & Standards

Engineered for the Accreditation Boundary

Public-sector compliance is not a checklist we attach at the end — it is a constraint we engineer to from day one. These four pillars run through every government engagement we deliver.

01

FedRAMP / FISMA

Cloud-workload and federal-system control alignment. We map our deployments to the relevant control baselines so your accreditation team is reviewing evidence, not chasing it.

02

Section 508 / WCAG

Accessibility verification before launch, not after. Screen-reader testing, keyboard nav, contrast checks, and semantic markup are part of the definition of done — never a post-launch retrofit.

03

Air-Gapped Deployment

Where sensitive missions require it, models run on-premises with no internet egress. Local inference, local audit logs, local key management — no data leaves the boundary.

04

Citizen Privacy

PII handling rules enforced at the data layer, retention policies wired into pipelines, FOIA-aware logging that captures what's needed and excludes what isn't.
