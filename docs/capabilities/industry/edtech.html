Industry Context

Education AI Has to Earn Trust Twice — From Learners and From SMEs

EdTech AI faces a double review: subject-matter experts validate quality before content reaches learners, and learners experience the output. Both audiences have low tolerance for hallucination and low patience for generic AI output.

Add FERPA for any system touching learner records, COPPA for under-13 audiences, region-specific privacy laws (EU/UK/Brazil), and accessibility (WCAG, Section 508) — and "GPT-wrapped" content tools fail every gate.

Our EdTech engagements ship review pipelines, SME approval workflows, and FERPA-aware data handling alongside the AI itself. The content is good. The compliance is correct. The learners use it.

Compliance & Standards

Engineered Into the Pipeline, Not Bolted On

Education compliance is layered. Our EdTech engagements ship with these as engineering requirements, not contractual disclaimers.

01

FERPA

Education record protection, learner-data minimization, role-based access scoping, and full audit trails for any system touching student records.

02

COPPA

Under-13 data handling, parental consent flows, content moderation, and verifiable age gates for products serving young learners.

03

WCAG / Section 508

Accessibility-first AI output: screen reader compatibility, keyboard navigation, ARIA labels, and structured content for assistive tech.

04

Regional Privacy

EU/UK/Brazil/India regional data residency, GDPR-aligned learner consent, and parental consent variations per jurisdiction.
