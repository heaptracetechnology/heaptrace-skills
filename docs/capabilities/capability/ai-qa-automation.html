What It Is

AI QA Is Not "Use ChatGPT to Write Tests"

Generic AI Test Generators Break Instantly

Paste a function into ChatGPT, ask for tests, get back code that looks right and breaks the moment you run it. The selectors are wrong. The mocks don't match your fixtures. The flow ignores your auth model. The tests pass on the LLM's imaginary version of your app — not yours. Generic AI test generators don't understand your domain, your repo, or the difference between a meaningful assertion and a placeholder.

Production AI QA Is an Engineering Practice

Real AI-driven QA requires four things working together: domain-tuned generation that knows your codebase and conventions; self-healing locators that survive UI churn; regression awareness that distinguishes real failures from flake; and CI/CD integration that turns the suite into a release gate, not a Slack-noise generator. None of that comes from a prompt — it comes from a delivery practice.

We Have the Team, the Engineers, and the Patterns

Our in-house QA automation specialists have run test suites at production scale. Our AI engineers have shipped LLM-driven systems that have to work, not just demo. And we bring the regression patterns we've collected across years of SaaS, fintech, and regulated-industry delivery — including playbooks for flake reduction, coverage backfill, and AI-powered triage that our clients use to ship faster without breaking trust.

How We Build It

The Five Pillars of Production AI QA

Every Heaptrace AI QA engagement delivers all five. Skipping any one of them is how you end up with a brittle suite that nobody trusts.

01

Domain-Tuned Test Generation

Train on your codebase, your test patterns, your domain language. Generated tests fit your repo, follow your conventions, and use your fixtures — not generic boilerplate that has to be hand-edited before it runs.

02

Self-Healing Locators

When a button moves, the test doesn't break. Locators auto-resolve from semantic intent and DOM similarity, so a UI refactor doesn't trigger a Monday morning of red builds and stale assertions.

03

Visual Regression at Scale

Screenshot diffs with smart noise filtering. No more 200 false positives per PR from anti-aliasing changes or font-rendering jitter. Component-aware diffs surface real visual regressions, not pixel hash collisions.

04

Coverage Gap Analysis

Code paths without test coverage flagged automatically. Risk-prioritized backfill suggestions tell you which untested branch to cover next based on commit churn, customer impact, and runtime exposure.

05

AI-Powered Failure Triage

Group failures by root cause, suggest fixes, distinguish flake from real regression. The first 30 minutes of a red build go from human triage to AI summary — engineers see the cause, not 80 failed assertions.
