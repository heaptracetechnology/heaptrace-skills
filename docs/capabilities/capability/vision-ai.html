What It Is

Vision AI Where the Stakes Are Real

Consumer image AI is forgiving — enterprise vision is not. A missed defect on a production line costs millions. A missed finding in a radiology study risks lives. The accuracy bar, the audit bar, and the deployment bar are all an order of magnitude higher than what "model accuracy on a benchmark" implies.

Production vision systems require versioned models, drift monitoring, audit trails, and edge deployment options. They have to keep working when lighting shifts, when document layouts change, when a new defect class appears, and when regulators come asking how a decision was made. None of that is a one-time training run — it's an engineering discipline.

Our team has shipped HIPAA-grade medical imaging, manufacturing defect detection at scale, and multi-region OCR. We know the difference between a Kaggle notebook and a regulated deployment — and we build the latter.

Our Approach

Five Pillars of Production Vision AI

Each pillar reflects an engineering practice we apply to every vision project — from pilot through scaled deployment.

01

Multi-Model Architecture

Best model per task. Detection, segmentation, classification, OCR — picked, not bundled. We avoid one-size-fits-all backbones in favor of architectures matched to the data, latency budget, and accuracy target.

02

Medical Imaging Pipelines

DICOM-native, PACS integration, HIPAA-compliant audit trails, regulated-region data residency. Built for diagnostic providers who need to defend every prediction in front of physicians and regulators.

03

Edge Deployment Ready

Models compiled for on-prem, NVIDIA Jetson, and mobile targets. Real-time inference where required — with the same versioning, monitoring, and audit story as cloud deployments.

04

Active Learning Loop

Misclassifications routed back to the label queue. Models improve with deployment, not despite it. Every production prediction becomes a candidate training signal — reviewed, weighted, retrained.

05

Compliance-Grade Audit

Every prediction logged with model version, confidence score, and input hash. Reproducible. Defensible. The kind of audit trail that holds up under HIPAA review, FDA scrutiny, or production-incident postmortems.
