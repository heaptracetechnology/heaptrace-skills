What It Is

Where RPA Stops, Agents Start

RPA automated the easy stuff — fixed, deterministic, single-system steps. Click here, copy there, paste into the next form. Useful, but bounded.

The workflows that actually consume time at the enterprise are messy: cross-system, exception-heavy, human-in-loop, and don't fit neat decision trees. They span legal review, security questionnaires, finance approvals, vendor data, and three Slack threads no one can find later.

We engineer agentic workflow systems that handle real cross-functional B2B processes — procurement, onboarding, claims, multi-step approvals — with audit-grade traceability, durable long-running execution, and exception handling treated as a first-class concern, not an afterthought.

How We Engineer It

Five Pillars of Agentic Workflow Engineering

Every workflow we ship gets all five. Skip any one and the system fails in production within 30 days.

01

Cross-System Orchestration

One workflow, multiple systems (Salesforce, SAP, Slack, Jira, custom apps). Agents reason across them, not just relay between them.

02

Human-in-Loop Checkpoints

Confidence thresholds and policy gates trigger human review. Slack, Teams, and email handoffs built in — not bolted on.

03

Long-Running Execution

Workflows that span days, weeks, or months without state loss. Durable orchestration, not flaky scripts that die on a deploy.

04

Exception Handling First-Class

Real workflows have edge cases. Exception queues, retry strategies, manual override paths — engineered, not bolted on later.

05

Audit-Grade Traceability

Every decision, every system call, every human approval logged with context. Defensible to procurement, legal, and compliance.
