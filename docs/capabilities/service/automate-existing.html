The Why

Working Systems, Manual Drag. We Add the Automation, Not the Risk.

Most enterprises don't need a new platform. They need their existing platforms to do more with less. Claims still go through claim ops, invoices still flow through AP, support tickets still route through your help desk — but the manual workload is eating margin and people.

Automate Existing is a targeted engagement model. We pick the bottleneck, drop in agentic AI alongside your existing systems, and ship measurable improvement in weeks, not quarters. No rewrite. No platform migration. No rip-and-replace.

The before/after metrics are the deliverable.

How It Runs

5 Stages, 16 Weeks Max

Predictable cadence. Tight scope. Each stage has a checkable output before the next one starts.

01

Week 1: Discovery

Pick the bottleneck. Measure baseline. Map the manual steps and the systems they touch — output is a one-page workflow doc with quantified pain.

02

Week 2-3: Design

Agent architecture and integration plan. Tool list, escalation thresholds, audit requirements, success metrics — signed off before any build starts.

03

Week 4-10: Build

Agent built and integrated alongside existing systems. Versioned, instrumented, regression-tested — we ship in increments your team can review weekly.

04

Week 11-14: Production Pilot

Live in production, measured against baseline. Real volume, real exceptions, real cost numbers — we tune thresholds and harden edge cases as data comes in.

05

Week 15-16: Handover

Documented, handed over, before/after report delivered. Your team has runbooks, dashboards, and access — or you extend into Team Retention from here.

Best-Fit Bottlenecks

Where Drop-In Agents Pay Off Fastest

Four categories where Automate Existing consistently delivers measurable ROI in a single 16-week cycle.

01

Document-Heavy Workflows

Claims, invoices, contracts — anywhere humans manually read PDFs, extract fields, validate against business rules, and key data into another system.

02

Multi-System Routing

Support tickets, exception management, escalation chains — work that bounces across CRM, ticketing, email, and chat before it lands with the right owner.

03

High-Volume Content Review

Compliance review, brand review, marketing QA — large batches of content checked against policy or guidelines, with reviewer fatigue driving inconsistency.

04

Repetitive Decision-Making

Eligibility checks, approval routing, tier classification — rules-based decisions that scale linearly with headcount and stall under volume spikes.
